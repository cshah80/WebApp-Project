Ransomware, Great Scott!

ransomware

What's so bad about ransomware? Is it really as bad as you've been lead to believe? Turns out, you were not lied to. It might even be worse than your worst nightmare. Ransomware is bad, outright malicious; it's designed to cause harm to individuals and organizations by encrypting their files and demanding payment in exchange for a decryption key. Ransomware attacks can result in a significant loss of critical data, damage to computer systems, and disruptions to business operations. If you still have uncontrollable urges to click on unsolicited links from unknown sources, don't try to resist too much. Your friendly neighborhood cyber security professional will get you out of any trouble you get into.

Cryptography, You're the Best, Around!

cryptography

You like secrets? Who doesn't? How do you keep your information private, safe from the spies all around us? Cryptography, that's how! Allow me to elaborate. Everywhere you look, everywhere you go, people are trying to steal your personal information. People you don't even know exist are trying to read the messages you send. Your life is too interesting to just put out there from anyone to see. Cryptography allows you to encrypt your data into an unreadable format to prevent unauthorized access. Cryptography provides for secure and reliable communication and data protection. It's an essential tool for maintaining privacy, so use it liberally in all aspects of your life.
